# usherin making — Brand Voice Guidelines

> A small Okinawa studio making frames of couples, families, and the days in between.

**Primary archetype:** The Companion (quiet, warm, present)
**Secondary archetype:** The Creator (craft-led, considered)

The studio is not a luxury fashion brand, a wedding hype machine, or a content farm. It is a *quiet, thoughtful service* that styles and photographs important days — and the voice should feel the same way the photographs do.

---

## Tone attributes

| Attribute | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| **Gentle** | *We style the day, find the light, and let the rest happen gently.* | *We capture your EPIC wedding story!* |
| **Specific** | *Susuki field, autumn.* / *Low tide, 17:40.* | *Beautiful outdoor scenes.* |
| **Spare** | *Love, softly kept in frames.* | *Your fairytale romance, immortalized forever.* |
| **Rooted** | *Made in Okinawa.* / *Fukugi tunnel, Bise.* | *Dream destinations worldwide!* |
| **Patient** | *By appointment. Year-round, island-wide.* | *Book now — spots filling fast!* |

## Voice rules

1. **Fragments are allowed — encouraged, even.** `Love, softly kept in frames.` beats a complete sentence when the idea is already arriving. Let line breaks do punctuation work.
2. **Use precise place names.** *Miyako, Naha, Kerama, Bise, the east coast.* Places carry memory. Generic location words ("tropical paradise", "beautiful island") don't.
3. **Use times and light as nouns.** *Blue hour. 17:40. Golden hour. Midday reef.* Light is the studio's real subject — name it directly.
4. **Korean, Japanese, and English coexist.** `어서린메이킹 スタイリング & スナップ` belongs on the page alongside the English. Don't hide any of the three.
5. **Never hype.** No *"stunning"*, *"breathtaking"*, *"magical"*, *"epic"*, *"unforgettable"*. If the photo is stunning the viewer will know.
6. **"We" is small, not corporate.** "We style and we photograph — gently." Studio-of-two voice, not agency-of-forty voice.
7. **End gently.** Close sentences with a soft verb, not a CTA shout. *"…let the rest happen gently."* / *"…kept gently."*
8. **Numbers belong in tag form.** `N° 01`, `No. 02 · Family`, `Ed. 04 — MMXXVI`. Reads like a journal, not an invoice.

## Type & layout voice

- **Script (Italianno)** — reserved for the wordmark only. Never for body copy, never for headers.
- **Serif italic (Fraunces)** — reserved for the accented words inside a headline (*softly*, *gently*, *quietly*). Sage color.
- **Serif roman (Fraunces)** — headlines and statement paragraphs.
- **Sans (Inter, all caps, wide-tracked)** — UI, labels, nav, meta lines. Small sizes only.
- **Noto Sans KR / JP** — Korean and Japanese characters only. Never force Latin serifs to render Hangul.

## Writing prompts (templates)

Use these when writing new copy.

**Journal caption (max 6 words):**
- `{Place} · {time-of-day}` → *Tidepool · Miyako* / *Hibiscus · Naha*
- `{Object} · {color}` → *Bouquet · Kerama blue*
- `{Action}, {time}` → *Low tide, 17:40*

**Section opener (max 2 lines):**
- `{Two people / A season / A morning}, {adverb}.` → *Two people, one island.* / *A season, well kept.*

**Micro-paragraph (40–65 words):**
Open with a bodily detail. One concrete place. One soft verb. Close with a closing gesture.

> *Couple snaps along the coast, through pampas fields and hibiscus paths. We style the day, find the light, and let the rest happen gently.*

## Words we use

`gently · softly · quiet · warm · kept · small · slow · island · light · season · frame · along · between · well`

## Words we avoid

`luxury · elite · exclusive · premium · premier · epic · stunning · breathtaking · unforgettable · magical · dream · perfect · best-in-class · unrivaled · unleash · capture (as a verb)`

## Evidence (baseline reading)

Run on the live homepage copy:
- Readability: **58 / 100** (middle — good for mixed international audience)
- Avg sentence length: **5.8 words** (intentionally short)
- Sentence variety: **high**
- Detected tone: **friendly**

Keep future copy within that envelope: 5–12 word average, high variety, friendly, fragment-heavy.
